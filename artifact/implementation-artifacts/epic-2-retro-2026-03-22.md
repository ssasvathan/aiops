## 1) Epic Review

### Epic Summary and Metrics

- Epic: **Epic 2 - Durable Case Artifacts and Reliable Action Dispatch**
- Story completion: **5/5**
- Completion rate: **100%**
- Functional scope delivered: FR26-FR35 (durable triage casefiles, guarded outbox transitions, concurrency-safe outbox publishing, denylist-safe external dispatch, lifecycle retention purge)
- Quality gate trend observed in story records: **890 -> 904 -> 914 -> 927 -> 934 passed, 0 skipped**
- Review findings addressed across the epic: multiple Critical/High/Medium/Low findings fixed before each story reached `done`

### Successes and Strengths

- Invariant A enforcement remained strong: triage artifact existence and integrity checked before downstream publication.
- Outbox durability patterns were hardened end-to-end: source-state transitions, row-locking with `FOR UPDATE SKIP LOCKED`, and at-least-once publish lifecycle.
- Security posture improved materially: denylist enforcement became explicit at PagerDuty and Slack boundaries.
- Observability and governance improved: lifecycle purge telemetry, failure visibility, and governance controls were tightened.
- Team consistently closed review findings with explicit test evidence instead of deferring quality debt.

### Challenges and Growth Areas

- Artifact traceability hygiene repeatedly needed review-level correction (file lists, stale comments/docs, evidence synchronization).
- Some reliability and safety checks were discovered late in review rather than prevented earlier in implementation.
- Process consistency still relied on reviewer vigilance more than automated completion checklists.

### Key Insights and Learnings

1. Source-state-guarded transitions plus SQL locking are the backbone of reliable outbox behavior in concurrent runtime conditions.
2. Centralized denylist enforcement and structured fallback logging materially reduced outbound safety risk.
3. Quality outcomes improved when acceptance criteria were converted into explicit tests early, not interpreted late.
4. Governance controls need to be validated as strict invariants at constructor/config boundaries, not only at downstream execution boundaries.
