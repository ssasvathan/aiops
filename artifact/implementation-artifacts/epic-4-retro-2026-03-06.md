## Deep Story Analysis (Stories 4-1 to 4-7)

### What Went Well

1. Durability chain was established end-to-end.
   - CaseFile assembly, write-once persistence, outbox state machine, and crash-safe publish flow now form a coherent path.

2. Integrity and safety controls improved materially.
   - Hash/checksum validation, denylist enforcement, and governed retention behavior are now explicit and test-backed.

3. Technical review discipline stayed strong.
   - High/medium findings were addressed quickly with focused fixes and tests.

4. Cross-story architecture continuity held.
   - Story outputs composed cleanly across storage, outbox, and integration boundaries.

### Challenges and Friction Points

1. Review-loop churn remained noticeable.
   - Many stories needed second-pass corrections for edge cases, transition guards, or metadata consistency.

2. Integration environment variability persisted.
   - Some integration paths were skipped in constrained environments and required ongoing guard handling.

3. Process artifacts lagged implementation rigor.
   - Team behavior improved, but explicit readiness/checklist templates are still not consistently codified.

### Pattern Synthesis

- Recurring feedback themes:
  - deterministic invariants,
  - typed failure handling,
  - explicit state-transition guards,
  - stronger negative-path and integration coverage.

- Breakthrough moments:
  - Story 4.4 durable Postgres outbox state machine,
  - Story 4.5 dedicated outbox-publisher recovery loop,
  - Story 4.6 publish-boundary denylist hardening,
  - Story 4.7 governed retention lifecycle.

- Collaboration highlight:
  - Adversarial review plus rapid remediation repeatedly produced measurable quality gains.
