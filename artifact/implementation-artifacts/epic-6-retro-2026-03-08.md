## Deep Story Analysis (6.1-6.4)

### What Went Well

1. Foundational sequencing worked: stub/failure-injection first (6.1) enabled safe, deterministic development for the cold path.
2. Hot-path independence was implemented with clear architectural boundaries in 6.2 (fire-and-forget tasking, bounded invocation criteria).
3. Structured diagnosis output with evidence citation and hash-chain persistence (6.3) significantly improved traceability.
4. Failure-mode hardening in 6.4 pushed reliability quality up: targeted exception handling, deterministic fallback, write-once persistence in failure paths.
5. Zero-skip quality gate culture held through the epic.

### Challenges and Friction

1. Import-boundary and layering issues reappeared during implementation/review and required corrections.
2. Failure-classification details in 6.4 needed iterative review to avoid misclassifying internal validation faults.
3. Persistence coverage for all failure branches required additional test expansion late in the story.
4. Process sequencing drift: Epic 7 appears in progress while Epic 6 is not fully closed.

### Pattern Synthesis

- Recurring review themes:
  - exception classification precision
  - edge-path test completeness
  - explicit architecture-boundary enforcement
- Breakthrough moments:
  - 6.2 made LLM invocation safely non-blocking for hot-path invariants
  - 6.3 added structured report + triage hash chain + write-once diagnosis stage
  - 6.4 unified deterministic fallback behavior across timeout/unavailable/error/schema-invalid scenarios
