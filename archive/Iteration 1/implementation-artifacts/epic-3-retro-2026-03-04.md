## Deep Story Analysis (Stories 3-1 to 3-5)

### What Went Well

1. **Foundation-first sequencing worked.**
   - Loader canonicalization (3.1) enabled stable resolver/routing work in 3.2–3.4 and compat projection in 3.5.

2. **Deterministic safety semantics held under review pressure.**
   - Unresolved behavior stayed explicit; no silent fallback patterns were introduced.

3. **Regression discipline remained strong.**
   - High/medium review findings were generally resolved with concrete tests and quality-gate reruns.

4. **Architecture boundaries were preserved.**
   - `registry/` and Stage 3 ownership remained clear; downstream coupling was minimized.

### Challenges and Friction Points

1. **Edge-case defects were still found late (during review).**
   - Pattern: malformed inputs, duplicate-key validation, and unresolved branches were often tightened post-review.

2. **Story documentation and implementation state drifted at times.**
   - Some story artifacts needed correction to reflect actual status/file changes.

3. **Review churn was avoidable.**
   - Back-and-forth reduced throughput even when fixes were narrow and fast.

### Pattern Synthesis

- **Recurring feedback themes:**
  - fail-fast validation,
  - explicit unresolved semantics,
  - stronger negative-path coverage,
  - deterministic output behavior.

- **Breakthrough moments:**
  - Story 3.1 established robust canonical loading and validation foundations.
  - Story 3.2/3.4 hardened scoped resolution + ownership routing with stronger adversarial cases.
  - Story 3.5 completed migration safety via canonical-to-v0 compatibility views without creating parallel data paths.

- **Collaboration highlight:**
  - Adversarial review was productive; fixes were generally targeted and traceable to tests.
